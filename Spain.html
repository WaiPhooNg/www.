Basic History 2

After multiple centuries and decades of development and conflict Spain became it's own nation and was a key enemy of England during most of history, they even sided with France during the Napoloenic wars until they were backstabbed by France and occupied. Ever since then Spain has been a fairly neutral country in history and has barely picked a side even during major events such as the second world war.

After the cold war Spain slowly shifted and switched away from their Axis-leaning ideology and became more and more democratic eventually. After becoming a democratic nation Spain sided with most of the western nations and joined NATO eventually.

Spanish cuisine

Spanish cuisine consists of the cooking traditions and practices from Spain. oil is heavily employed in Spanish cuisine. It forms the bottom of the many vegetable sauces. Herbs most typically used include parsley, oregano, rosemary and thyme. the utilization of garlic has been noted as "common to all or any Spanish cooking."
